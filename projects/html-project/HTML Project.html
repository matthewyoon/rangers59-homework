About Me

Hello world! My name is Matt and I was born and raised in Chicago but currently call Boston my home. I am a former healthcare worker turned hopefully into developer. I worked as an assistant in different private medical practices but during the COVID-19 pandemic decided to pursue a career that had always piqued my interest: coding. Ever since making the big decision to pursue coding, every day has been full of experiencing the unknown which is both a daunting and fulfilling journey. I hope to continue to learn and expand upon my budding knowledge.

Before committing to learning about data, python, and coding in general I wrestled with the idea of switching my career entirely and diving into an unknown world. It took literal months before I took the leap of faith. That being said, I'm eagerly anticipating this next adventure in my life and I just hope I learn some cool things, meet new people, and can celebrate small successes along the way.

Hobbies

I love filling up my free time with things that clear my head and keep me busy. Some examples include cooking, playing games, working out, and more. Lately I've been really into making traditional Japanese ramen with a pork-based tonkotsu broth. It's a long but simple process that keeps my mind and hands busy.

The bowl on the left is my very first iteration of ramen which includes a tonkotsu broth, soft-boiled egg, char-siu pork, scallions, and store bought noodles. The other bowl is my most recent creation that added a lot more depth and flavor which includes a spicy miso tare, corn, bean sprouts, black garlic oil, a quarter of a sheet of nori(seaweed), and even handmade egg noodles using an old-fashioned crankable pasta machine. Can you tell I love food?

A certain pasttime that seemed to shake the world during the pandemic is a videogame called Call of Duty: Warzone. I'm simultaneously proud and not proud of the time I've put into the game, but at the very least, I have something to show for the time that I've logged in the game. At just about the top 1% of all registered players in the world, I think it's pretty cool. Needless to say, videogames are something I enjoy and will probably continue to enjoy, albeit in a more moderate fashion, for the rest of my life.
